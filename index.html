About me

I am a student of Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Guangzhou), majoring in Robotics and Autonomous Systems, in Transportation Behavioral Psychology and Safety Lab (TBPS).

Research interests include Human-computer Interaction in Autonomous Driving, Human Factors, Accessibility Design, Social Computing and Network Analysis.

My previous work was in ByteDance and Netease Interactive Entertainment as a product manager, designing dealer middle-end systems, and customer service chatbot, Identifying risks and abnormal transactions through data.

What i'm doing

    Click to see the game demo

    Using VR to increase the frequency and improve the quality of communication between the elderly and their children.

  • Lightweight Crowd Counting

    We propose a lightweight deep learning approach for accurate crowd counting, maintaining high efficiency. By compressing the CNN and Transformer models simultaneously, we transfer knowledge from a state-of-the-art teacher model, achieving excellent performance. Experimental results confirm the success of our knowledge distillation methods, reducing the parameter size to less than one-tenth of the teacher model while maintaining comparable accuracy.

  • Marvel Network Analysis

  • Driving Ahead: A Study on the Impact of Training Methods for Anticipatory Driving

    This research project aims to identify what specific knowledge is required for novice drivers to develop anticipatory driving skills, and how do different types of training methods impact the development of these skills quantitatively.
